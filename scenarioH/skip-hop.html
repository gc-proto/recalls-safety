Skip Hop Tuo convertible high chairs - Front legs can break

Skip Hop

Recall date: January 9, 2018

Summary

  • Product: Skip Hop Tuo Convertible high chairs, sold between December 2016 and September 2017
  • Issue: Front legs can detach, causing a fall
  • What to do: Stop using it and contact Skip Hop for a free replacement

Affected products

This recall involves the charcoal colored Tuo Convertible High Chair, which can be converted into a toddler chair.

The only highchairs involved in this recall are the ones sold from December 2016 to September 2017 in Canada and the United States.

They have style numbers of 304200 and 304200-CN with these date codes on the back of the chair:

  • HH102016
  • HH11/2016
  • HH3/2017
  • HH4/2017

The highchairs have a reversible seat pad, removable tray, 5-point harness, beechwood footrest and legs.

Other chairs are not affected.

Issue

The front legs on the highchair can detach from the seat, posing a fall and injury hazard to children.

As of January 9, 2018, the company has received no reports of detached legs in Canada. In the United States, the company has received 2 reports of bruising.

What you should do

If you have this chair, you should immediately stop using it and contact Skip Hop for a free replacement.

For more information, consumers can contact Skip Hop by telephone toll-free at 1-888-282-4674 from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. ET Monday through Friday, by email or online and click on Product Recalls at the bottom of the page under Shopping With Us for more information.
